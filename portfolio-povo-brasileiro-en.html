The Musical

In 2016 the vocal group CONTRACANTOS earned an accomplishment through the call of the Pernambuco Culture Sponsorship Fund – FUNCULTURA and promoted a tour through the cities of Grenoble, Lyon, and Paris in France. With the purpose of spreading Brazilian culture, specifically the culture of the Northeast of Brazil, POVO BRASILEIRO: CANTOS DO NORDESTE was prepared for the French audience. The concert was presented also in the SESC School of Rio de Janeiro, in the SESC/PE VIII Mostra Leão do Norte (Garanhuns-PE), during the celebration of the 70th anniversary of the Federal University of Pernambuco at the Santa Isabel Theater (Recife), in the concert series Música no Hermilo at Hermilo Borba Filho Theater (Recife), and in the VIII International Music Festival of Campina Grande – FIMUS (Campina Grande-PB).

POVO BRASILEIRO: CANTOS DO NORDESTE is a musical concert with scenic elements that presents Brazilian culture through a repertoire of musical works from the Northeast of Brazil, portraying the different values that formed its people. The concert contains vocal and instrumental music, allied with elements of Cordel poetry, body gesture, and rhythms of Pernambuco. The musical is based on Northeast Brazil oral traditions of Aboios, Maracatu’s Toadas, Cirandas, Coco, Caboclinho, Frevo and others.

The performance reflects four moments in Northeast Brazil’s history, life, and music: The Armorial movement; beach chants; African-Brazilian chants; and the Carnival of Pernambuco. POVO BRASILEIRO: CANTOS DO NORDESTE exposes a picture of Brazil, using elements from the popular religious culture of the Northeast of Brazil Brincante (a person who plays a role in traditional culture).

The Ensembles

CONTRACANTOS was created in 2002 by choir conductor and professor at Federal University of Pernambuco, Flávio Medeiros. It has become one of the prominent vocal groups in the Northeast of Brazil. CONTRACANTOS has been giving concerts in theaters, churches, schools and cultural spaces, presenting in cities of Pernambuco as well as in other states of the country and outside Brazil in France, Switzerland, and United States. The choir has performed at festivals such as the Lotus World Music & Arts Festival (USA), La Fête de la Musique (France), in Pernambuco, Mostra Leão do Norte, Mostra Internacional de Música em Olinda, and the Virtuosi Internacional, Virtuosi Brasil and Virtuosi na Serra. The choir was featured on CD recordings with the Symphonic Orchestra of Recife, the group Sagrama, the Symphonic Orchestra Virtuosi, and the Federal University of Pernambuco Orchestra.

The group performs varied repertoire, from the classic to the popular, in concerts a capella as well as accompanied by orchestras or chamber groups, conducted by Flávio Medeiros and by invited Brazilian conductors such as Osman Gioia, Sílvio Barbato, José Renato Accioly, Dierson Torres, and Maria Aida Barroso, as well as foreign conductors Frank Abrahams (USA), Erick Westberg (Sweden), Christèle Rifaux (France), and Monica Alfaya (Argentina).

CONTRAVENTOS is the instrumental sextet made-up of musicians with backgrounds in classic and popular music. The group was created in 2013 to perform with CONTRACANTOS and is composed of flute, clarinet, bass-clarinet, horn, and percussion. It includes also traditional instruments of the Northeast of Brazil like the pifano, the berrante, the viola nordestina and the sanfona. This instrumentation allows new combinations and sounds, producing research, creation, and composition of new arrangements.
